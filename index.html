Data Science

Master of Science in Data Science

Unlock the power of data with the University of Regina's comprehensive Master of Data Science (MDS) program. Designed to equip you with interdisciplinary knowledge and practical experience, our two-year program combines rigorous coursework with real-world applications to prepare you for a successful career in data science.

Key Program Features

Duration

2 years (8 semesters)

Curriculum

Core courses, electives, professional development seminars, capstone project, and industry internship

Practical Experience

Integrated capstone project and 8-month industry internship

Flexibility

Elective courses to tailor your learning to specific interests

Professional Development

Seminars focused on enhancing soft skills and career readiness

Apply Now

Program Outcome

Upon completion of the MDS program, you will:
  • Develop Comprehensive Skills

    Master data analysis, machine learning, cloud computing, and understand ethical considerations in data science.

  • Gain Real-World Experience

    Apply your knowledge through a capstone project and an industry internship, tackling real-world challenges.

  • Be Industry-Ready

    Equipped with practical skills and professional development, you'll be prepared for a successful career in data science.

Curriculum Structure

Fall Semester

Foundations of Data Science

Introduces fundamental data science concepts, tools, and workflows essential for data analysis.

Programming for Data Science

Develops programming skills in Python and R focused on data manipulation and analysis.

Year One

Winter Semester

Statistical Methods and Probability

Covers basic statistical techniques and probability models used in data science.

Database Systems and Data Management

Teaches concepts of relational databases, SQL, and principles of effective data management.

Summer Semester

Admission Requirements

  • Academic Qualifications

    Bachelor's degree in computer science, statistics, mathematics, engineering, or a related field.

  • Prerequisite Knowledge

    Proficiency in programming and foundational mathematics.

  • English Language Proficiency

    Proof of proficiency in English for non-native speakers, such as IELTS or TOEFL scores.

  • Supporting Documents

    Academic transcripts, letters of recommendation, statement of purpose, and résumé or CV.

    • Fall Intake:   June 1
    • Spring Intake:   November 1
